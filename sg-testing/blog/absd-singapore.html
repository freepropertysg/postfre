What Is ABSD in Singapore?

ABSD, or Additional Buyerâ€™s Stamp Duty, is a tax paid on top of Buyerâ€™s Stamp Duty when purchasing residential property in Singapore. It was introduced to manage demand and maintain market stability.

ABSD is determined by:

  • Number of residential properties owned
  • Whether the buyer is an individual or company

Why ABSD Matters

ABSD significantly affects the upfront cost of property purchases. For second or third properties, the additional tax can be substantial.

Singapore ABSD Rates Overview

ABSD rates vary by buyer profile:

  • Singapore Citizens: No ABSD on first property, higher rates for additional properties
  • Singapore PRs: ABSD applies from the first property
  • Foreigners: Flat ABSD rate regardless of property count
  • Entities and Developers: Highest ABSD tier applies

How ABSD Affects Buyers and Investors

ABSD impacts affordability, cash flow and investment returns. Buyers often compare:

  • Upgrading versus buying an additional property
  • Decoupling options and costs
  • Investment versus own-stay decisions
  • New launch versus resale pricing

For investors, ABSD can reduce rental yield and extend breakeven timelines.

Ways to Reduce ABSD Exposure

Depending on eligibility, some buyers consider:

  • Buying under a single name
  • Decoupling (subject to rules and costs)
  • Purchasing commercial properties (no ABSD)
  • Selling before buying another residential unit

Always assess legal and financial implications carefully.
