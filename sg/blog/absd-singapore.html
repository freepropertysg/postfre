What is ABSD in Singapore?

ABSD, also known as Additional Buyerâ€™s Stamp Duty, is a tax payable on top of Buyerâ€™s Stamp Duty when purchasing residential property in Singapore. It was introduced to keep the property market stable and sustainable.

ABSD applies based on:

  • Buyer profile (SC, SPR, foreigner, entity)
  • Number of residential properties owned
  • Type of buyer (individual or company)

Why ABSD Matters

Understanding ABSD helps buyers know the true upfront cost of buying a property. For second or third properties, ABSD can add a large amount to the purchase price.

Singapore ABSD Rates

ABSD rates depend on residency status and property count. Below is a simplified overview:

  • Singapore Citizens: 0% for first property, higher rates for additional properties
  • Singapore PRs: ABSD applies from the first property
  • Foreigners: Flat higher rate regardless of property count
  • Entities and Developers: Highest ABSD tier applies

How ABSD Affects Property Buyers

ABSD affects affordability, investment return, and long-term planning. Many buyers compare:

  • Upgrading versus buying a second property
  • Decoupling options
  • Investment versus own-stay decisions
  • New launch versus resale costs

For investors, ABSD can reduce rental yield and extend the breakeven period.

How to Reduce or Avoid ABSD

Depending on eligibility, some legal methods may reduce ABSD exposure:

  • Buying under a single name
  • Decoupling (subject to rules and costs)
  • Buying commercial property (no ABSD)
  • Selling before buying another residential unit

Always assess risks and costs before proceeding.
